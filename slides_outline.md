## SLIDE 2: THE PROBLEM — WHY WE NEEDED A NEW THEORY *[1 minute]*

**The Old Story: Required Reserves**
- Fed raises rates → reduces reserves → banks lend less  
- But this stopped being realistic decades ago  
- Since 2008: reserves are abundant and earn interest  

> The bank lending channel had evidence — but no credible mechanism.

**DSS’s Answer:**  
Banks’ **market power over deposits** is the missing link.

---

## SLIDE 3: THE CORE IDEA — INTUITION FIRST *[1 minute]*
Fed Funds Rate ↑
↓
Banks widen deposit spreads
↓
Deposits flow out (to bonds, money markets)
↓
Banks lose funding
↓
Lending contracts → economy slows

### Why can banks do this?

- Depositors are **sticky**:
  - inattention  
  - switching costs  
  - preference for safety  

👉 Simple intuition:
> When rates are high, people don’t chase deposit rates as much → banks have more pricing power.

> **Market power increases when rates rise.**

## SLIDE 5: IDENTIFICATION — HOW DO WE KNOW IT’S CAUSAL? *[1.5 minutes]*

**Main challenge:**  
Maybe deposits fall because loan demand falls — not market power.

---

### DSS strategy: Same bank, different markets

| Branch A | Same Bank | Branch B |
|---|---|---|
| Competitive market | ← same lending → | Concentrated market |

- Same bank → same lending opportunities  
- Only difference = **local competition**

---

### Key result (per 100 bps hike):

- High-concentration branches:
  - +14 bps higher spreads  
  - −66 bps larger deposit outflows  

---

👉 Don’t focus on the equation — the idea is:

> Same bank behaves differently depending on competition → that’s causal evidence.

## SLIDE 7: FROM DEPOSITS → LENDING → ECONOMY *[1 minute]*

**Why does this matter?**

Deposits are:
- cheap  
- stable  
- insured  

They are hard to replace.

---

### Result:

- When deposits fall → lending falls  

---

### Key number:

- 100 bps rate hike →  
  **−291 bps lending** (for high market power banks)

---

### Aggregate effect:

- 400 bps cycle →  
  - **$1.35 trillion drop in deposits**  
  - **$763 billion drop in lending**

---

## SLIDE 8: CRITICAL ASSESSMENT *[1 minute]*

### Strengths

- Very clean identification  
- Strong evidence across multiple datasets  
- Solves a major theoretical gap  

---

### Limitations

- Data ends in 2013 → **pre-fintech**
- Online banking reduces switching costs  
- Assumes representative depositor  
- Doesn’t capture cases like SVB  

---

## SLIDE 9: REAL-WORLD RELEVANCE *[1 minute]*

### 2022–2023 rate hikes

- Deposits ↓ ~**$1 trillion**  
- Money market funds ↑ ~**$1.2 trillion**  

✔ Matches DSS predictions  

---

### Silicon Valley Bank

---

## SLIDE 10: CONCLUSION *[30 seconds]*

**What the paper shows:**

- Monetary policy affects:
  - deposit pricing  
  - deposit quantity  
  - bank lending  

---

### Final takeaway:

Monetary policy doesn’t just affect borrowing —  
it affects the **supply of deposits**.

And that depends on **how much power banks have over depositors**.

---

> As that power changes, the entire transmission mechanism may change too.
